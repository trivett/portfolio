I am a journalist and photographer currently based in New York City.  I write about technology, politics, global migration, international affairs, finance and economics.

In 2011, I graduated from a masters program in business reporting and interactive journalism at CUNY's Graduate School of Journalism. Until mid-2010, I lived in Yokohama, Japan where I wrote for the Yokohama Seasider Magazine, Koe Magazine, and the Japan Beer Times. I speak Japanese fluently. I am interested in photography, video, and data visualization. Right now, I am busy studying programming with Ruby and Ruby on Rails.

My work has appeared in Business Insider, AM New York, City Limits, WNYC, Minyanville, and the Atlantic Business Blog.

You can read my curriculum vitae for a more detailed summary of my professional life. You can connect to me via Twitter, LinkedIn, Flickr, and Google + as well.
